Once I slept in a surfboard bag on a grassy dune, overlooking a city beach in Durban, South Africa. Durban is not exactly the safest place in the world to sleep outside. But I knew the waves would be pumping at sunrise. I hardly slept. Dawn was dark grey before strands of gold crept up over the horizon. I could hear waves breaking and could feel the light, offshore breeze. I placed my t-shirt in my surfboard bag and tied it to a metal railing on the pier that separates North Beach from the Bay of Plenty. I didn’t jump off the pier, as I usually did. I’m not sure why. Maybe because it was still dark. I ran back to the beach and paddled out. You hardly need to paddle because the rip beside the pier is like a funnel that ejects you into the line-up. Of course I was alone out there, besides the fish and the… best not think about that. The sun opened its eye and turned the water to honey. A wave rose up right where I was waiting. I turned and paddled, dropping down onto a glassy wall that stretched out like elastic. Instead of racing or turning I simply looked down. The sun’s fire blazed through the green ocean wall, like an incandescent, living spirit. The Wave, by Jules Phillips, from an article on Surfd.com I rode that wave to the shore, then ran back along the beach as fast as I could. Three more golden waves peeled across the bay, each a little more perfect than the last. By the time I was back out there I was beaming, fulfilled, thoughtless (free of mental chatter), completely in the moment, totally alive. So, yes, I’d say surfing is fun. 1.5K viewsView Upvoters
